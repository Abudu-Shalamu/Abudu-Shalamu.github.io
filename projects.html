P.A.G.E.S. —
Picture books As Gateways to European Society

P.A.G.E.S. leverages picture books as a powerful "universal language" to transcend linguistic barriers and foster deeper understanding of major societal issues — such as human rights, gender diversity, and war trauma — from a European perspective.

Aligned with the EU 2030 Agenda, this project orchestrates a series of interactive workshops at the University of Padova and the University of Wrocław. It redefines picture books not merely as children's literature, but as sophisticated tools for university-level critical thinking, enabling students from diverse backgrounds to "read" visual narratives as sites of cultural negotiation and healing.

BIBI —
Biographies and Biofiction of Childhood

The BIBI project (Biografie e Biofiction d'Infanzia) is a comprehensive national research and educational intervention programme led by the University of Padova. It is designed to promote gender respect, deconstruct stereotypes, and foster female empowerment through the critical analysis of childhood biographies and biofiction.

Moving beyond traditional literary analysis, BIBI operates as a catalyst for social change. It investigates how the "storied lives" of diverse figures can offer young readers alternative role models. As a core team member, I contribute to the theoretical framework that connects visual narratives with gender pedagogy, developing strategies that transform passive reading into active social awareness.
